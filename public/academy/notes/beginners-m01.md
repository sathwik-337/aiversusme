## Page 1: What exactly is Artificial Intelligence?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a branch of computer science that aims to create machines capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence.

### 1.1 The Definition of AI
Intelligence includes skills like:
- **Reasoning**: Using logic to solve problems.
- **Learning**: Improving performance based on past experience.
- **Perception**: Understanding the world through sight (images) or sound (speech).
- **Language**: Communicating through text or voice.

### 1.2 AI vs. Standard Software
- **Traditional Software**: Follows a strict set of rules written by a human. (e.g., "If the button is clicked, change the color to red.")
- **AI Software**: Learns the rules from data. (e.g., You show the computer thousands of pictures of cats, and it learns to recognize a cat on its own.)

### 1.3 Why is AI so popular now?
Three things happened at the same time:
1. **Big Data**: We now have massive amounts of information from the internet.
2. **Powerful Computers**: Computers are now fast enough to process all that data.
3. **Better Algorithms**: Scientists discovered better ways to teach computers.

## Page 2: The Different Types of AI

Not all AI is the same. Scientists usually divide AI into three levels of capability.

### 2.1 Narrow AI (ANI - Artificial Narrow Intelligence)
This is the AI we use today. It is designed to do **one specific task** very well.
- **Examples**: Siri, Netflix recommendations, Google Maps, and FaceID.
- **Limitation**: A chess-playing AI cannot drive a car or write a poem.

### 2.2 General AI (AGI - Artificial General Intelligence)
This is a machine that can perform **any intellectual task** that a human can.
- **Status**: This does not exist yet. It is still the subject of research and science fiction movies.
- **Capability**: An AGI could learn to play chess, then learn to cook, then learn to write code, just like a person.

### 2.3 Super AI (ASI - Artificial Super Intelligence)
This is a hypothetical AI that is **smarter than all of humanity combined** across every field.
- **Status**: Purely theoretical. Scientists debate whether this will ever happen.

## Page 3: A Brief History of AI

The idea of "thinking machines" is not new. It has been a human dream for centuries.

### 3.1 The 1950s: The Beginning
- **Alan Turing**: The "father of AI" who asked the famous question: "Can machines think?"
- **1956 Dartmouth Workshop**: The moment the term "Artificial Intelligence" was born. Scientists were very optimistic and thought they would solve AI in just a few years.

### 3.2 The AI Winters
The field went through periods called "AI Winters" where funding was cut because AI didn't live up to the hype.
- **The Problem**: Computers were too slow and didn't have enough memory.

### 3.3 The Modern Era (2010 - Present)
- **The Deep Learning Revolution**: In 2012, a computer program showed it could recognize objects in pictures better than anything before it.
- **Generative AI (2022)**: The release of ChatGPT made AI accessible to everyone, not just scientists.

## Page 4: AI in Your Daily Life

You are probably using AI dozens of times every day without even realizing it.

### 4.1 On Your Phone
- **Facial Recognition**: Unlocking your phone with your face.
- **Predictive Text**: Your keyboard guessing the next word you want to type.
- **Virtual Assistants**: Siri, Alexa, and Google Assistant.

### 4.2 On the Web
- **Social Media**: TikTok and Instagram showing you videos they think you'll like.
- **Shopping**: Amazon suggesting products based on your past purchases.
- **Search**: Google using AI to understand the *meaning* of your search, not just the keywords.

### 4.3 In the World
- **Medicine**: AI helping doctors spot cancer in X-rays.
- **Finance**: Banks using AI to detect if your credit card has been stolen.
- **Transportation**: Apps like Uber using AI to predict traffic and set prices.

## Page 5: Summary and Next Steps

### 5.1 Module Summary
- AI is about making machines smart by letting them learn from data.
- We currently live in the era of **Narrow AI**.
- AI has a long history of ups and downs but is now growing faster than ever.
- AI is already a part of almost every app and service we use.

### 5.2 Common Myths about AI
- **Myth**: AI is going to take over the world tomorrow.
- **Fact**: AI is a tool created and controlled by humans.
- **Myth**: AI is a "magic box" that knows everything.
- **Fact**: AI is only as good as the data it was trained on.
